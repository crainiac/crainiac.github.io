On Sonnets and Kolmogorov Compressors

June 15, 2024

Sonorous sonnets bloom as bluebonnets,
Flawlessly sprawling each phrase upon its
Blossoming tapestry of majesty
Without strictly speaking of travesty
When to do so would dull by rote let-on
Those thorns that imply a far sharper con,
As in a sin-pro sort of shallow praise,
Outlining emptiness in purple phrase.
I prefer chatspeak unkempt and succinct,
Straight, to-the-point, digital and distinct;
Logographers reformed Homeric song;
I find the form of the sonnet too strong,
And long for lit zingers, words infodense:
